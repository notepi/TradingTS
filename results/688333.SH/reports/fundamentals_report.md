**Key Takeaways:**

Using the 2025 earnings-express figure lowers trailing P/E to 116.81x, but the absolute level remains high. Given that the company is still in an expansion phase, with sizable quarterly earnings volatility and only moderate cash conversion, P/B and P/S are relatively more stable and better suited to tracking the path from capital investment to revenue expansion to profit realization. ## **Final Assessment**

### **Overall Conclusion: Expansion phase; operations improving but cash flow remains under pressure; valuation sits between lower historical levels and mid-range, but absolute valuation remains high**

**Rationale:**
BLT is currently in a fairly typical “expanding manufacturing growth stock” phase. Revenue and profit improved materially in 2025 versus 2024, especially with much stronger quarterly profitability in Q2 and Q3, indicating that expansion into new application areas and growth in customized civil-market products are beginning to show through in earnings. At the same time, however, operating cash flow remains weaker than accounting profit, inventory and receivables have risen quickly, capital expenditure remains high, and borrowings have increased. From a valuation perspective, current P/E, P/B, and P/S have all compressed significantly from earlier high-expectation periods if viewed against history; however, in absolute terms, TTM P/E is still above 100x. This means the market still assigns a relatively high long-term growth valuation. Whether this valuation can be sustained or further re-rated will depend less on continued revenue growth alone and more on improvement in earnings quality, cash conversion, and capex payback efficiency.

**Key Points to Watch:**

1. Whether the formally disclosed 2025 annual report differs materially from the earnings express.
2. Whether operating cash flow, accounts receivable, and inventory show simultaneous improvement in the 2026 Q1 report.
3. Whether the growth in fixed assets and construction in progress from expansion continues to translate into higher revenue and better gross margin.
4. Subsequent developments in the accounting restatement and regulatory case, and whether they affect financial comparability and market risk appetite.

**Risk Reminder:**
This report is based on public filings, earnings express data, exchange disclosures, and market quotation data.
